DRACO: Data-driven Embodied Agent for Closed-loop Collaborative Optimization of Robotic Perception and Manipulation

CVPR 2025 Anonymous Submission

DRACO is an embodied skill-learning agent based on realistic perception within a closed-loop framework. ASR indicates the average success rate. Comparison on previous frameworks focus on (a) generation and (b) skill training, either simply default that perception is perfect, or manually design scenarios. Our agent (c) performs manipulation based on realistic perception and uses a PMO module to optimize strategies in a closed loop, and also by generating unlimited embodied skill simulation scenes in a task-driven manner, our approach enhances the generalization capability of the policy.

Abstract

Training embodied skills in real-world settings faces high costs, safety issues, and complex constraints. While training in simulators using generated data offers a cost-effective alternative, existing attempts often assume perfect perception and lack closed-loop feedback. We find that when considering perception error, these policies failed to generalize either on trained or unseen scenarios. To this end, we propose DRACO, a data-driven closed-loop framework for collaborative optimization of robotic perception and manipulation. We use a proposed PMGen module to automatically generate unlimited 3D simulation scenes, providing diverse simulated scenarios to enhance the efficiency and accuracy of skill training. DRACO also presents a novel perception-manipulation collaborative optimization module (PMO), which refines the control-based policy using a vision-based supervisor policy, forming a closed-loop system to improve task success rates and policy generalization. We first benchmark our framework on a door-opening scenario. Compared to the baseline, the results show that our framework achieves an average success rate improvement of 80.1% on the training set and 40.0% on the unseen scenes after data scaling. Moreover, we extend our framework on various tasks and result in an average success rate of 81.9%, yielding the generalization ability of our approach. We further deploy the pretrained policy by our framework on a real embodied agent to validate the sim-real transferability.
